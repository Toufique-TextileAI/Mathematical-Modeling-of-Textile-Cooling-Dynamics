# Mathematical Modeling of Textile Cooling Dynamics

## 📌 Overview

This repository contains the mathematical modeling and thermodynamic analysis of cooling curves for a three-layer outerwear system. Specifically, it evaluates the heat dissipation dynamics of a **Woven Jute Mid-Layer** and a **Recycled Polyester Inner Layer** using transient temperature decay data.

The Python notebook applies multiple mathematical models to empirical sensor data to determine the optimal fit for the distinct physical and structural properties of each textile layer. It goes beyond basic $R^2$ metrics by employing rigorous **Residual Analysis** to prove model suitability and prevent underfitting.

## 📊 Dataset

The analysis expects two datasets containing raw thermal sensor data (Time in seconds vs. Temperature in Kelvin):

* `Jute.csv`: Cooling data for the 1.3 mm woven jute mid-layer.
* `synthetic.csv`: Cooling data for the 0.125 mm recycled polyester inner layer.

*(Note: Data loading handles `latin1` encoding to seamlessly process special characters like degree symbols generated by the thermal testing hardware).*

## 🧮 Mathematical Models Evaluated

To map the physical dual-phase thermodynamics (surface convection vs. internal conduction) and complex thermal resistance, the following models are fitted to the data:

1. **Linear Model** ($y = mx + c$)
2. **2nd-Order Polynomial Regression**
3. **Single Exponential Decay** ($T(t) = a \cdot e^{-bt} + c$)
4. **3rd-Order Polynomial Regression** (Optimal for thick, porous insulators)
5. **Double Exponential Decay** (Optimal for dual-phase synthetic cooling)

## 🛠️ Key Features & Methodology

* **Curve Fitting:** Utilizes `scipy.optimize.curve_fit` and `numpy.polyfit` to fit thermodynamic equations to the sensor data.
* **Goodness-of-Fit Metrics:** Calculates $R^2$ (Coefficient of Determination), **RMSE** (Root Mean Square Error), and **MAE** (Mean Absolute Error) using `scikit-learn`.
* **Residual Analysis:** Generates precise residual plots to visually identify systematic bias (e.g., U-shaped underfitting) and validate that the mathematical models match the fundamental physics of the fabrics.
* **High-Resolution Visualization:** Uses `matplotlib` to export publication-ready $4 \times 2$ grid figures comparing all alternative models and their residuals side-by-side.

## 🏆 Key Findings

* **Woven Jute Mid-Layer:** Best modeled by a **3rd-Order Polynomial**. The mathematical flexibility of this equation successfully captures the complex, uneven, multi-modal thermal resistance caused by the thick (1.3 mm), highly porous cellulosic structure.
* **Synthetic Polyester Layer:** Best modeled by a **Double Exponential Decay** equation based on Newton's Law of Cooling. This perfectly maps the rapid initial convective heat loss coupled with the slower internal conductive phase.
